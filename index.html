Harlem Public Space Redesign Proposal

Montefiore Square

Reimagining the community plaza at Broadway, Hamilton Place, and West 138th Street as a cooler, more flexible, and more fully activated public space for neighborhood life in Harlem.

View the Proposal Directions via Google Maps

Site Overview

Broadway, Hamilton Pl., W. 138 St.

Manhattan, New York City

About the Square

Montefiore Square is a neighborhood plaza and park in Harlem that includes an open lawn, terraced stone seating, perennial planting, and a paved plaza designed for both everyday use and public events.

What Already Works

The 2021 renovation improved the square’s layout, expanded the footprint, and created stronger landscape elements that support everyday community use.

Why Redesign Again?

This proposal builds on the recent redesign by identifying what still limits the plaza and proposing a second layer of improvements focused on comfort, activation, and long-term community use.

Problem Definition

What still needs to change after the 2021 renovation

The 2021 redesign created real improvements, but three gaps still limit Montefiore Square’s potential as a fully activated community destination.

Shade Gap

The newly planted trees are still young and do not yet provide enough canopy cover. During summer afternoons, the central paved zone remains exposed to direct sun, making the plaza uncomfortable during the hours when public outdoor space is most needed.

Seating Limitation

The terraced stone seating is visually strong but not fully comfortable. It offers no back support, limited flexibility, and no ability to move with shade or social activity. For longer stays, more adaptable seating is needed.

Activation Infrastructure Gap

The plaza supports community events in principle, but lacks the permanent physical infrastructure needed for regular programming, including a small stage, electrical access, and vendor support space.

Existing Site Features

Strong foundations already exist at Montefiore Square

Open Lawn

A central green space supports rest, informal activity, and daily neighborhood use.

Terraced Stone Seating

Durable seating edges frame the lawn and create structure for gathering and observation.

Perennial Garden

The planting design softens the plaza and adds seasonal color, biodiversity, and visual comfort.

Paved Plaza

The hardscape zone has the potential to support performances, markets, and community events.

Flexible Public Use

The square already supports both quiet daily use and more active forms of neighborhood life.

Neighborhood Identity

Montefiore Square serves as a visible community landmark and civic space in Harlem.

Design Proposal

Three moves to complete the plaza

This redesign is organized around what the Montefiore Park Neighborhood Association needs to support everyday use, stewardship, and regular programming.

1. Shade Infrastructure

Install two to three seasonal tensile shade sails over the central paved plaza to provide immediate thermal relief during peak summer hours while the newly planted trees continue to mature.

2. Moveable Seating

Add 50–60 weatherproof café chairs and 15 small tables that complement the existing stone terraces, allowing people to choose sun or shade, orient toward performances, or gather socially.

3. Stage & Market Infrastructure

Build a small permanent stage with power access and provide two vendor kiosk zones along Broadway so the plaza can regularly support markets, performances, and community events.

Design Options

Comparing two directions

Option A — Shade & Comfort First

This option focuses on thermal comfort and flexible seating through shade sails, moveable chairs, and supplemental planting.

Strength: Quick, visible, and cost-effective.

Limitation: Does not provide infrastructure for regular performances or vendor activity.

Option B — Community Activation Hub (Selected)

This option includes all comfort improvements from Option A and adds a small permanent stage and vendor infrastructure to support community programming.

Strength: Supports both daily use and organized events.

Why selected: It directly supports the Neighborhood Association’s stewardship work and long-term programming goals.

AI Reflection

How AI was used in this project

AI tools were used to help visualize the redesign through concept imagery and to assist in organizing some design ideas into a clearer proposal structure.

However, the most important part of this project did not come from AI. Identifying the Montefiore Park Neighborhood Association, understanding their role in advocating for the 2021 redesign, and recognizing their long-term stewardship work required independent research and human judgment.

AI helped generate possibilities, but it could not understand the deeper community context of the site. The final design direction was chosen based on the question of what the Association needs in order to activate the plaza more fully, not just what would look visually improved.

Community Stewardship

Local care and long-term advocacy

The Montefiore Park Neighborhood Association assists NYC Parks in maintaining this sloping triangle and plays a central role in the life of the space.

The Association advocated for the 2021 redesign, coordinates gardening, supports community involvement, and organizes events. This proposal is designed as a direct response to the work they are already doing.

Why this matters

  • Builds on existing stewardship, not abstract design ideas
  • Supports the people already caring for the space
  • Turns occasional programming into regular community activation
  • Strengthens local identity, ownership, and long-term use

Closing Vision

Montefiore Square should become a cooler, more comfortable, and more active community plaza.

This proposal completes the work of the 2021 renovation by adding the missing layer of comfort and activation infrastructure needed for everyday neighborhood life and public programming in Harlem.
